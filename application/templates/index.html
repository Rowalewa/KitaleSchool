About Us

Kitale School is one of the few pre-colonial learning institutions in the country. The institution was set up by the colonial government under the sponsorship of the Church Mission Society, current-day ACK. Started 90 years ago, Kitale School has withstood the test of time to maintain high education standards in the region. Located about two kilometres from Kitale town, the school sits on 70 acres and is among five pre-colonial learning institutions in the country. Others are Nairobi School, Molo School, the Hill School in Eldoret and Nyeri Primary School. When Sir Edward Grigg became governor in 1925, he provided funds for building the school which admitted white learners only. The agricultural depression of the 1930s and the locust invasion that caused extensive crop damage spelt doom for the town, adversely affecting the growth of the school. It was not until the end of World War II that the deployment of British army soldiers boosted the fortunes of Kitale town, positively impacting on the school's growth. Businessman Mogul Paresh Tanna was the first Asian child to enrol in 1962 after the institution opened its doors to non-whites when Kenya gained independence in 1963.
